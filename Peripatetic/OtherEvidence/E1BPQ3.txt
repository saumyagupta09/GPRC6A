CC   -!- FUNCTION: Receptor activated by multiple ligands, including osteocalcin
CC       (BGLAP), basic amino acids, and various cations. Activated by amino
CC       acids with a preference for basic amino acids such as L-Lys, L-Arg and
CC       L-ornithine but also by small and polar amino acids. The L-alpha amino
CC       acids respond is augmented by divalent cations Ca(2+) and Mg(2+). Seems
CC       to act through a G(q)/G(11) and G(i)-coupled pathway. Regulates
CC       testosterone production by acting as a ligand for uncarboxylated
CC       osteocalcin hormone: osteocalcin-binding at the surface of Leydig cells
CC       initiates a signaling response that promotes the expression of enzymes
CC       required for testosterone synthesis in a CREB-dependent manner.
CC       Mediates the non-genomic effects of androgens in multiple tissue. May
CC       coordinate nutritional and hormonal anabolic signals through the
CC       sensing of extracellular amino acids, osteocalcin, divalent ions and
CC       its responsiveness to anabolic steroids.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8K4Z6}.
