## Figure 2. CDD Domain Hit for the GPRC6A Exon 4 Protein

## Figure 3. Three-Frame Codon Alignment of Human and Bovine GPRC6A Exon 4

![Figure 3](Figure_3.png)

This multi-line alignment maps *Homo sapiens* exon 4 against all three prospective forward reading frames (+1, +2, and +3) of *Bos taurus*. Codons are locked vertically with their respective amino acids, and identities are marked by a match axis (`|`). The target mass-spectrometry peptide (**"NDVFIVTNQETK"**) is highlighted in **bold red**. The alignment demonstrates that only Reading Frame +3 contains the target peptide barcode and shares an evolutionary frame conservation with humans; frames +1 and +2 introduce early stop codons (`*`) and sequence mismatches.

## Figure 4. Transcription Start Sites and Enhancer Annotations at the GPRC6A Locus

![Figure 4](Figure_4.png)

NCBI Genome Data Viewer (GDV) view of the **GPRC6A** genomic locus. Tracks include NCBI RefSeq gene annotations and the **GPRC6A orthologous exons** track, which highlights *Bos taurus* exons orthologous to those of other mammals. Orthologous exons 1–6 are indicated by green, orange, pink, blue, red, and brown colours, respectively.

Transcription start sites (TSSs) are displayed from three sources: **Promoter 2.0** predictions, **CAGE-seq-derived TSSs** reported in a previous study, and **NCBI GDV TSS** tracks representing 5′ end counts of read alignments from CAGE-seq experiments. CAGE-seq-derived TSS tracks are shown separately for individual tissues and strands. Putative enhancers identified from CAGE-seq data are displayed in the **CAGE-seq Enhancers.bed** track.

## Figure 10. Exon-wise Comparison of GPRC6A RNA Abundance

![Figure 10](Figure_10.png)

Box plots showing the distribution of mean RNA abundance for **GPRC6A** exons 1–6 across all available tissues in *Bos taurus*, *Sus scrofa*, and *Homo sapiens*. The X-axis represents individual exons, and the Y-axis represents RNA mean abundance.

For each exon, three box plots are shown: *B. taurus* (left, pink), *S. scrofa* (middle, blue), and *H. sapiens* (right, green), as indicated in the legend. Boxes represent the interquartile range (IQR), with whiskers extending to the minimum and maximum observed values. Summary statistics displayed within each box include the minimum and maximum abundance (red), median (green), mean (blue), and sample size (**N**, black), calculated across all analysed tissue samples.

Pairwise species comparisons were performed independently for each exon using Welch's two-sample *t*-test followed by Benjamini–Hochberg false discovery rate (FDR) correction. Adjusted *p*-values and significance levels are displayed above each exon as brackets connecting the corresponding species pairs. Dark blue brackets indicate statistically significant differences, whereas grey brackets indicate non-significant comparisons (**ns**).
